This puzzle consists of a list of clues and a word search. We may start with the thought that we need to solve the clues and then find the words in the word search, so we should begin with looking at the clues. However, after some inspection, we realize that a single word is insufficient to answer some of these clues. Some easier answers that will hint us towards this includes “WHITE CHRISTMAS”, “YELLOW PAGES” and “BLUE WHALE”. Once we identify a few of them, we realize that these are all phrases that consist of a color + a related word (note that some of these have the color as the second word).

Once the list of clues is solved, we naturally proceed to find the words in the word search. (In reality, we intend for teams to use the clues and grid simultaneously, attempting to find word segments in the grid that will help them solve the harder clues as they progress in the puzzle.) Noticing that the answers are ordered alphabetically in the order of the related word, we infer that this is the word we must find in the search grid.

At first if we treat it as a normal word search, we realize that some of these words occur multiple times in the grid, some do not occur, and there are other words that seem to occur in the grid! However, if we start from the top right or bottom right corners we can get an idea of how the grid is meant to be read. To find the words, we must notice that the grid is broken up into 8 x 8 squares, and words are written in row-major order outlining regions of the grid. After trying to identify and read off a couple of words, we find that the words associated with one color are given in row-major order in that particular region. This associates every cell with a color, and we can then color the grid in.

At this point it is relevant to recall that this puzzle is located at the dock, which reminds us that these are all pixel art of international maritime signal flags! Reading off the flags, we obtain the answer, YACHT TRAINEE.
